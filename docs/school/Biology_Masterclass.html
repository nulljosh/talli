Unit 1Homeostasis & Biological Molecules

Homeostasis

Definition: body maintaining internal balance despite external changes.

Mechanism: negative feedback loops — Receptors detect change → Control center processes → Effectors respond.

Examples: temperature regulation, blood glucose control, pH balance.

Water Properties

  • Polar molecule, partial charges; covalent bonds within, hydrogen bonds between molecules
  • Solvent, temperature moderator (high specific heat, evaporative cooling), cohesion/adhesion, transport medium

pH & Acids/Bases

  • 0–6 acidic, 7 neutral, 8–14 basic
  • Acids release H⁺, bases release OH⁻
  • Buffers resist pH change — crucial for enzyme function
  • Blood pH ≈ 7.4, stomach pH ≈ 2

Biomolecules

Macromolecule cheat sheet
Carbohydrates — monomers: monosaccharides; quick energy, structural support, energy storage
Lipids — triglycerides (long-term energy, insulation), phospholipids (membranes), steroids (hormones)
Proteins — monomers: amino acids; primary → secondary → tertiary structure; shape determines function
Nucleic acids — DNA/RNA, nucleotides (phosphate + sugar + base); store/transmit genetic info; ATP = energy currency
Memory hacks
  • CLPN — Carbs quick energy, Lipids long-term, Proteins do work, Nucleic acids store info
  • Organelles: "Never Call My Rough English, Go Learn Coding" (Nucleus, Cytoplasm, Mitochondria, Ribosomes, ER, Golgi, Lysosomes, Chloroplasts)

Unit 2Cell Structure & Function

Cell Theory

  1. All living things are made of cells
  2. Cells are the basic unit of life
  3. All cells come from pre-existing cells

Prokaryotes vs Eukaryotes

Prokaryotes: no nucleus, less efficient (bacteria). Eukaryotes: have nucleus, more efficient (plants, animals).

Organelles & Functions

Both
  • Nucleus — control center, DNA
  • Mitochondria — ATP
  • Ribosomes — protein factories
  • ER — transport (rough has ribosomes)
  • Golgi — processes/packages proteins
  • Cytoskeleton — shape
Animal only
  • Lysosomes — digest waste
  • Centrioles — cell division
  • Small vacuoles — storage
Plant only
  • Chloroplasts — photosynthesis
  • Cell wall — structure/protection
  • Large vacuole — storage/support

Membrane Transport

Passive (no energy): diffusion, osmosis, facilitated diffusion.

Active (requires energy): primary (ATP direct), secondary (gradients), endocytosis, exocytosis.

Tonicity: isotonic — no net movement; hypertonic — cell shrinks; hypotonic — cell swells.

Memory hacks
  • "Water follows salt" for osmosis
  • "HYPE-rtonic makes cells shrink from HYPE-rdehydration"
Foundation warning

If you don't understand transport, every other unit gets harder — this connects to everything.

Unit 3DNA, Protein Synthesis & Enzymes

DNA Structure & Replication

  • Double helix; base pairing A-T, C-G; phosphate + deoxyribose + nitrogenous base
  • Semi-conservative replication: Helicase unzips, DNA polymerase adds nucleotides, Ligase seals fragments

RNA & Protein Synthesis

RNA differs from DNA: single strand, ribose sugar, uracil instead of thymine. Types: mRNA (messenger), tRNA (transfer), rRNA (ribosomal).

Central dogma
Transcription (nucleus): RNA polymerase reads DNA template → mRNA
Translation (ribosomes): Initiation → Elongation (tRNA brings amino acids) → Termination (stop codon, protein released)

Mutations

Point mutations: silent (no effect), missense (different amino acid), nonsense (premature stop). Frameshift: insertion/deletion shifts reading frame.

Enzymes

  • Biological catalysts, lower activation energy; lock-and-key active site
  • Affected by temperature (denaturation past optimum), pH, concentration
  • Inhibition: competitive (competes for active site) vs non-competitive (binds elsewhere, changes shape)
Memory hacks
  • Base pairs: "Apple Tree, Car Garage" (A-T, C-G)
  • Central dogma: "DNA makes RNA makes Protein" (DRP)
  • Replication: "Helicase Helps, Polymerase Produces, Ligase Links"
  • Restaurant analogy: DNA = recipe book, mRNA = order ticket, tRNA = waiters, ribosome = kitchen

Unit 4Digestive System

Pathway: Mouth → Esophagus → Stomach → Small intestine → Large intestine → Rectum.

  • Mechanical digestion: physical breakdown (chewing, churning)
  • Chemical digestion: enzyme breakdown — amylase (starch), pepsin (protein), lipase (fats)
  • Absorption mainly in small intestine, optimized via villi
Memory hack

"My Mouth Eats Small Lunch Regularly" — trace the pathway first, then layer in enzymes/absorption.

Unit 5Respiratory System

  • External respiration: gas exchange in lungs (O₂ in, CO₂ out)
  • Internal respiration: gas exchange at tissues
  • Alveoli: tiny air sacs for gas exchange; hemoglobin carries O₂
  • Medulla monitors CO₂ levels and adjusts breathing rate
Memory hack

BRIN — Breathe, Ribs expand, Internal gas exchange, Nose/mouth entry.

Unit 6Circulatory System

  • Heart: 2 atria (receive blood), 2 ventricles (pump blood)
  • Blood flow: right side → lungs → left side → body
  • Arteries: away from heart, thick walls, high pressure. Veins: return to heart, valves, lower pressure. Capillaries: thin walls, gas/nutrient exchange
  • Blood: RBCs (O₂ transport), WBCs (immune), platelets (clotting), plasma (liquid)
Memory hacks

"Right Ventricle Leaves, Left Ventricle Arrives"; "Arteries Away, Veins Venture back".

Unit 7Nervous System

  • CNS: brain and spinal cord. PNS: all other nerves
  • Neurons: electrical signals within cell, chemical signals between cells
  • Reflexes: automatic protective responses
  • Brain processes sensory info and controls responses
Memory hack

"CNS Controls, PNS Provides".

Unit 8Excretory System

  • Filters blood, removes wastes, maintains water/salt balance
  • Nephrons: functional units of kidneys
  • Process: Filtration → Reabsorption → Secretion → Excretion
  • Hormones: ADH (water retention), aldosterone (salt retention)
Memory hack

"Kidneys Keep Blood Clean".

Unit 9Reproductive System

  • Male: testes produce sperm and testosterone
  • Female: ovaries produce eggs, estrogen, progesterone
  • Menstrual cycle: hormone-controlled monthly cycle
  • Fertilization in fallopian tube; development: embryo → fetus → baby
Memory hack

"Hormones Control Everything" in reproduction.

ReviewAll Flashcards

What are the 4 components of a negative feedback loop?
Stimulus → receptor → control center → effector → response
Why is water a good solvent?
It's polar, so it dissolves polar/ionic substances
What's the difference between passive and active transport?
Passive needs no energy (diffusion, osmosis, facilitated diffusion); active requires ATP
What happens to a cell in a hypertonic solution?
It shrinks — water leaves the cell
Name the 3 enzymes in DNA replication.
Helicase (unzips), DNA polymerase (adds nucleotides), Ligase (seals fragments)
Where do transcription and translation happen?
Transcription in the nucleus; translation at ribosomes in the cytoplasm
What's the digestive pathway in order?
Mouth → esophagus → stomach → small intestine → large intestine → rectum
What does hemoglobin do?
Carries oxygen in the blood
What are the heart's 4 chambers and their roles?
2 atria receive blood, 2 ventricles pump blood
CNS vs PNS?
CNS = brain + spinal cord; PNS = all other nerves
What are the 4 steps of kidney filtration?
Filtration → reabsorption → secretion → excretion
What hormones control the menstrual cycle?
Estrogen and progesterone
